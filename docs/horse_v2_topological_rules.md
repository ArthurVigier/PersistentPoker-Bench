# PersistentPoker-Bench H.O.R.S.E. v2: Topological Rule Specification

## Status And Scope

This document gives a mathematical translation of the PersistentPoker-Bench
`horse_v2` house variant. It is deliberately more formal than the user-facing
rules because its purpose is to make the game reproducible, analyzable, and
citable as a benchmark object.

The document has two layers:

- **Engine-conformant layer**: rules that describe the behavior currently encoded
  by the repository.
- **Idealized H.O.R.S.E. layer**: mathematical extensions that explain the intended
  semantics of names such as `omaha_8b` and `stud_8b` when the current engine is
  not yet fully split-pot authentic.

Unless explicitly marked as an idealized extension, statements below are intended
to define the engine-conformant benchmark rule.

## 1. Primitive Sets

### 1.1 Cards

Let

$$
R = \{2,3,4,5,6,7,8,9,T,J,Q,K,A\}
$$

be the set of ranks and

$$
S = \{\clubsuit,\diamondsuit,\heartsuit,\spadesuit\}
$$

be the set of suits. The single-deck card space is

$$
D = R \times S,
\qquad |D| = 52.
$$

Each physical hand is dealt from a permutation of \(D\). Across hands, however,
the persistent public pool is not a physical deck; it is a multiset. Therefore
the benchmark distinguishes:

$$
\text{physical cards in one hand} \in \mathcal{P}(D),
\qquad
\text{persistent pool} \in \mathbb{N}^{D}.
$$

The space \(\mathbb{N}^{D}\) is the free commutative monoid generated by \(D\).
For a pool \(P \in \mathbb{N}^{D}\), \(P(c)\) is the multiplicity of card
\(c \in D\). Duplicate card identities are legal in \(P\).

The pool size is the \(L^1\) norm:

$$
\|P\|_1 = \sum_{c \in D} P(c).
$$

### 1.2 Players And Seats

For \(n\) players, define the seat set

$$
I_n = \{0,1,\dots,n-1\}.
$$

Seats form a directed cycle

$$
C_n = \mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z},
$$

with successor map

$$
\sigma(i) = i + 1 \pmod n.
$$

The active, non-folded, non-eliminated seat subset at state \(x\) is denoted

$$
L(x) \subseteq I_n.
$$

The set of seats still able to make a betting decision is

$$
A(x) \subseteq L(x).
$$

### 1.3 Topological Convention

All primitive game components are finite or countable discrete spaces. The
complete state space is a product of such spaces, hence it carries the product
topology. Since finite factors are discrete, every singleton state is open.

The topological interest is not local continuity in the analytic sense. It is
the structure induced by:

- quotient maps from complete state to player-observable state,
- fibers representing information sets,
- filtrations representing information revealed over time,
- stratification by game variant and street,
- transition maps between strata.

This lets the benchmark be treated as a finite topological dynamical system with
imperfect information.

## 2. Variant Space And Rotation

### 2.1 Variant Set

The H.O.R.S.E. v2 variant space is

$$
V =
\{
H,\ O8,\ Rz,\ St,\ S8
\},
$$

where

$$
H = \text{Texas Hold'em},
$$

$$
O8 = \text{Omaha Hi-Lo 8-or-better label},
$$

$$
Rz = \text{Razz},
$$

$$
St = \text{Seven Card Stud High},
$$

$$
S8 = \text{Seven Card Stud Hi-Lo 8-or-better label}.
$$

Engine labels are:

$$
H \mapsto \texttt{holdem},
\quad
O8 \mapsto \texttt{omaha\_8b},
\quad
Rz \mapsto \texttt{razz},
\quad
St \mapsto \texttt{stud},
\quad
S8 \mapsto \texttt{stud\_8b}.
$$

### 2.2 Rotation Map

Let hands be indexed by

$$
h \in \mathbb{N}_{\ge 1}.
$$

The rotation block size is

$$
b = 8.
$$

Let

$$
\rho = (H,\ O8,\ Rz,\ St,\ S8)
$$

be the cyclic ordering. The current variant is the map

$$
\operatorname{var}(h)
=
\rho_{\left\lfloor \frac{h-1}{b} \right\rfloor \bmod 5}.
$$

Equivalently, hand blocks are:

$$
B_k = \{kb+1,\dots,(k+1)b\},
\qquad
\operatorname{var}(h) = \rho_{k \bmod 5}
\quad \text{for } h \in B_k.
$$

Topologically, \(\operatorname{var}\) is a locally constant map from the
discrete hand-time space \(\mathbb{N}_{\ge 1}\) to the finite cyclic variant
space \(V\).

## 3. Street Posets And Alexandrov Topology

Each variant has a finite street poset. The topology used for reveal order is
the Alexandrov topology induced by the partial order.

For flop games \(H,O8\):

$$
\mathsf{Street}_{F}
=
\{
\mathsf{preflop}
<
\mathsf{flop}
<
\mathsf{turn}
<
\mathsf{river}
<
\mathsf{showdown}
\}.
$$

For stud games \(Rz,St,S8\):

$$
\mathsf{Street}_{S}
=
\{
\mathsf{third}
<
\mathsf{fourth}
<
\mathsf{fifth}
<
\mathsf{sixth}
<
\mathsf{seventh}
<
\mathsf{showdown}
\}.
$$

The reveal filtration is monotone:

$$
\mathcal{F}_{t}
\subseteq
\mathcal{F}_{t'}
\quad
\text{whenever }
t \le t'.
$$

This expresses that public information is never unrevealed inside a hand.

## 4. Complete State Space

At hand \(h\) and decision epoch \(k\), the complete state is

$$
x_{h,k}
=
\left(
v,\ t,\ \pi,\ K,\ U,\ B,\ P,\ s,\ m,\ m^{street},\
\beta,\ r,\ a,\ Q,\ F,\ E,\ All,\ Hist,\ Deck
\right).
$$

The coordinates are:

$$
v \in V
$$

the current variant;

$$
t \in \mathsf{Street}_{F} \cup \mathsf{Street}_{S}
$$

the current street;

$$
\pi \in I_n
$$

the button index;

$$
K = (K_i)_{i \in I_n}
$$

private down-card sets or tuples;

$$
U = (U_i)_{i \in I_n}
$$

visible up-card tuples;

$$
B \in D^{\le 5}
$$

the board for flop games;

$$
P \in \mathbb{N}^{D}
$$

the persistent public pool carried from previous hands;

$$
s \in \mathbb{N}^{I_n}
$$

current stacks;

$$
m,\ m^{street} \in \mathbb{N}^{I_n}
$$

total commitments and current-street commitments;

$$
\beta \in \mathbb{N}
$$

current table bet;

$$
r \in \mathbb{N}
$$

last full raise size;

$$
a \in I_n
$$

the actor index;

$$
Q \subseteq I_n
$$

the ordered pending-actor set;

$$
F,E,All \subseteq I_n
$$

folded, eliminated, and all-in seats;

$$
Hist
$$

the public action history;

$$
Deck \in D^{\le 52}
$$

the unrevealed suffix of the shuffled physical deck.

The legal state space is the subset

$$
X \subseteq
V \times \mathsf{Street} \times I_n
\times \prod_i D^{\le 4}
\times \prod_i D^{\le 4}
\times D^{\le 5}
\times \mathbb{N}^{D}
\times \mathbb{N}^{I_n}
\times \mathbb{N}^{I_n}
\times \mathbb{N}^{I_n}
\times \mathbb{N}^2
\times I_n
\times 2^{I_n}
\times 2^{I_n}
\times 2^{I_n}
\times 2^{I_n}
\times Hist
\times D^{\le 52}
$$

satisfying the dealing, betting, and street-consistency constraints below.

## 5. Public And Private Information Topology

### 5.1 Public Observation Map

The public observation map is

$$
\Pi_{pub}: X \to Y
$$

where

$$
\Pi_{pub}(x)
=
\left(
v,\ t,\ \pi,\ U,\ B,\ P,\ s,\ m,\ m^{street},\
\beta,\ a,\ Q,\ F,\ E,\ All,\ Hist
\right).
$$

It forgets all down-cards \(K_i\) and unrevealed deck cards.

### 5.2 Player Observation Map

For player \(i\), the observation map is

$$
\Pi_i: X \to O_i
$$

with

$$
\Pi_i(x)
=
\left(
\Pi_{pub}(x),\ K_i
\right).
$$

The information set of player \(i\) at observation \(o_i\) is the fiber

$$
\mathcal{I}_i(o_i)
=
\Pi_i^{-1}(\{o_i\}).
$$

This fiber is a subspace of \(X\). The player's strategic problem is a decision
rule on the quotient topology induced by \(\Pi_i\):

$$
\delta_i: O_i \to \mathcal{A}.
$$

### 5.3 Persistent Pool Memory Metric

An LLM decision includes a claimed pool belief

$$
\widehat{P}_i \in \mathbb{N}^{D}.
$$

The true pool is \(P\). A natural benchmark distance is

$$
d_{pool}(\widehat{P}_i,P)
=
\|\widehat{P}_i - P\|_1
=
\sum_{c \in D}
\left|
\widehat{P}_i(c) - P(c)
\right|.
$$

Precision, recall, and multiset accuracy are derived from the overlap

$$
\operatorname{overlap}(\widehat{P}_i,P)
=
\sum_{c \in D}
\min(\widehat{P}_i(c),P(c)).
$$

This makes the persistent pool a topological memory load: as \(\|P\|_1\)
increases, the observation fiber is not larger in cardinality merely because of
the pool, but the cognitive representation required to identify the public
state becomes longer and more duplicate-sensitive.

### 6.3 Razz, Stud, Stud 8B

For

$$
v \in \{Rz,St,S8\},

## 7. Forced Bets And First Actor

### 7.1 Flop-Game Blinds

For \(H\) and \(O8\), define:

$$
SB(\pi) =
\begin{cases}
\pi, & |L| = 2, \\
\sigma(\pi), & |L| > 2,
\end{cases}
$$

and

$$
BB(\pi) =
\begin{cases}
\sigma(\pi), & |L| = 2, \\
\sigma(SB(\pi)), & |L| > 2.
\end{cases}
$$

The small blind and big blind commitments are:

$$
m_{SB} \gets m_{SB} + \min(s_{SB}, sb),
$$

$$
m_{BB} \gets m_{BB} + \min(s_{BB}, bb).
$$

The first preflop actor is:

$$
UTG =
\begin{cases}
\pi, & |L| \le 2, \\
\sigma(BB), & |L| > 2.
\end{cases}
$$

### 7.2 Stud Bring-In

For \(Rz,St,S8\), there is a bring-in seat \(b_{in}\).

For Razz, the intended rule is highest visible card brings in under A-to-5 low
ranking:

$$
b_{in}
=
\operatorname*{arg\,max}_{i \in L}
\operatorname{rank}_{A5}(U_i[0]).
$$

For Stud High and Stud 8B, the lowest visible card brings in:

$$
b_{in}
=
\operatorname*{arg\,min}_{i \in L}
\operatorname{rank}_{high}(U_i[0]).
$$

Ties are resolved deterministically by seat order unless an explicit dice
tiebreaker is introduced for bring-ins.

The bring-in commits:

$$
m_{b_{in}} \gets m_{b_{in}} + \min(s_{b_{in}}, sb).
$$

The first decision actor is:

$$
a = \sigma(b_{in})
$$

restricted to the next live, non-eliminated seat.

## 8. Legal Action Correspondence

The action alphabet is

$$
\mathcal{A}
=
\{
\mathsf{fold},
\mathsf{check},
\mathsf{call},
\mathsf{bet}(z),
\mathsf{raise}(z),
\mathsf{all\_in}
\}.
$$

Let

$$
c_i(x)=\max(0,\beta-m^{street}_i)
$$

be the amount to call for player \(i\), and

$$
M_i(x)=m^{street}_i+s_i
$$

be the maximum target commitment for the street.

The legal-action multimap is

$$
\Lambda: X \rightrightarrows \mathcal{A}.
$$

For actor \(i=a(x)\):

$$
\mathsf{check} \in \Lambda(x)
\iff
c_i(x)=0.
$$

$$
\mathsf{call} \in \Lambda(x)
\iff
c_i(x)>0.
$$

$$
\mathsf{fold} \in \Lambda(x)
\iff
c_i(x)>0.
$$

$$
\mathsf{bet}(z) \in \Lambda(x)
\iff
\beta=0
\land
\max(bb,m^{street}_i+1) \le z \le M_i(x).
$$

$$
\mathsf{raise}(z) \in \Lambda(x)
\iff
\beta>0
\land
z \ge \beta+r
\land
z \le M_i(x)
\land
\text{raise action is reopened for } i.
$$

$$
\mathsf{all\_in} \in \Lambda(x)
\iff
s_i>0.
$$

Each legal action induces a partial transition map

$$
T_\alpha: X \dashrightarrow X.
$$

The transition is undefined when \(\alpha \notin \Lambda(x)\).

## 9. Betting-Round Closure

A betting round is complete at state \(x\) if:

$$
Q(x)=\varnothing
$$

and every non-folded, non-all-in player has matched the current table bet:

$$
\forall i \in I_n,\quad
i \notin F \cup All
\implies
m^{street}_i = \beta.
$$

It is also terminal if:

$$
|L(x)| \le 1.
$$

The closure predicate is:

$$
\operatorname{closed}(x)
=
\left[
Q=\varnothing
\land
\forall i \notin F \cup All,\ m^{street}_i=\beta
\right]
\lor
\left[
|L| \le 1
\right].
$$

## 10. Street Advancement Maps

When \(\operatorname{closed}(x)\) is true and the hand is non-terminal, the
engine applies a street transition

$$
\Gamma_v: X_t \to X_{t'}
$$

where \(t'\) is the successor street.

### 10.1 Hold'em And Omaha

For \(H,O8\):

$$
\mathsf{preflop} \to \mathsf{flop}: |B| \gets 3,
$$

$$
\mathsf{flop} \to \mathsf{turn}: |B| \gets 4,
$$

$$
\mathsf{turn} \to \mathsf{river}: |B| \gets 5,
$$

$$
\mathsf{river} \to \mathsf{showdown}.
$$

At each street transition:

$$
\beta \gets 0,
\qquad
m^{street}_i \gets 0,
\qquad
\text{acted}_i \gets \bot.
$$

The next actor is the first live, non-all-in seat after the button.

### 10.2 Stud, Razz, Stud 8B

For \(Rz,St,S8\):

$$
\mathsf{third} \to \mathsf{fourth}: |U_i| \gets |U_i|+1,
$$

$$
\mathsf{fourth} \to \mathsf{fifth}: |U_i| \gets |U_i|+1,
$$

$$
\mathsf{fifth} \to \mathsf{sixth}: |U_i| \gets |U_i|+1,
$$

$$
\mathsf{sixth} \to \mathsf{seventh}: |K_i| \gets |K_i|+1,
$$

$$
\mathsf{seventh} \to \mathsf{showdown}.
$$

Only live players receive new street cards.

The intended Stud/Razz first actor after third street is the best exposed board.
The current engine-conformant behavior chooses the first active non-all-in seat.
This is a known simplification of the topological transition map.

## 11. Public Reveal As A Filtration

Let

$$
\mathcal{F}_{h,t}^{pub}
$$

be the public sigma-algebra generated by the public observation at hand \(h\)
and street \(t\). Because the spaces are finite, this is equivalently the
partition of \(X\) induced by \(\Pi_{pub}\).

For a fixed hand:

$$
\mathcal{F}_{h,t}^{pub}
\subseteq
\mathcal{F}_{h,t'}^{pub}
\quad
\text{if } t \le t'.
$$

For the match across hands, the persistent pool induces:

$$
\mathcal{F}_{h,\mathsf{showdown}}^{pub}
\to
\mathcal{F}_{h+1,\mathsf{initial}}^{pub}
$$

by carrying \(P_{h+1}\) into the next hand.

Thus, the whole match is a path in a stratified state space:

$$
x_{1,0}
\to x_{1,1}
\to \cdots
\to x_{1,K_1}
\to x_{2,0}
\to \cdots
\to x_{H,K_H}.
$$

## 12. Persistent Public Pool Operator

The pool update has two phases:

1. Append visible cards.
2. Apply winner metacognitive action.

Let

$$
W_h \in \mathbb{N}^{D}
$$

be the visible-card contribution from hand \(h\).

For \(H,O8\):

$$
W_h = \operatorname{multi}(B_h).
$$

For \(Rz,St,S8\):

$$
W_h
=
\sum_{i \in I_n}
\operatorname{multi}(U_{i,h}).
$$

This is the custom H.O.R.S.E. rule that makes Stud-family hands especially
memory-heavy: all exposed up-cards enter the long-lived public pool.

The intermediate pool is:

$$
P_h^+ = P_h + W_h.
$$

The winner declares

$$
\delta_h \in \{\mathsf{reset},\mathsf{continue}\}.
$$

The next pool is:

$$
P_{h+1}
=
\begin{cases}
0, & \delta_h=\mathsf{reset},\\
P_h^+, & \delta_h=\mathsf{continue}.
\end{cases}
$$

If no valid winner decision is available, the default is:

$$
\delta_h=\mathsf{continue}.
$$

## 13. Winner Pool Decision Function

Let \(Win_h \subseteq I_n\) be the set of winning seats at hand \(h\). Each
winner may have emitted a pool decision in the transcript:

$$
\delta_{h,i} \in \{\mathsf{reset},\mathsf{continue}\}
\quad
\text{for } i \in Win_h.
$$

If all observed winner decisions agree, the hand decision is their common value.

If there are conflicting winner decisions, the engine uses a deterministic
D6 tiebreaker. For contenders \(C \subseteq I_n\), round \(r\), and context
label \(q\), define:

$$
Roll(i,r,q)
\in
\{1,2,3,4,5,6\}
$$

as the pseudorandom die roll derived from:

$$
(seed,\ hand\_id,\ q,\ r,\ C).
$$

Survivors after round \(r\) are:

$$
C_{r+1}
=
\left\{
i \in C_r:
Roll(i,r,q)=\max_{j \in C_r} Roll(j,r,q)
\right\}.
$$

Rounds continue until \(|C_r|=1\).

## 14. Showdown Evaluation Spaces

Let

$$
Eval_{high}: \mathbb{N}^{D} \to \mathcal{K}_{high}
$$

be the custom PersistentPoker high-hand evaluator. Its ordering is lexicographic:

$$
Eval_{high}(M)
=
(\operatorname{category}(M),\operatorname{dup}(M),\operatorname{value}(M)).
$$

The category order from strongest to weakest is:

1. Double Royal Flush
2. Five of a Kind
3. Double Straight Flush
4. Royal Flush
5. Straight Flush
6. Four of a Kind plus Flush
7. Four of a Kind
8. Full House plus Flush
9. Flush
10. Straight
11. Three of a Kind
12. Two Pair
13. One Pair
14. High Card

The topological viewpoint treats this evaluator as an order-preserving map from
the finite multiset state space into a totally ordered comparison space.

## 15. Variant-Specific Showdown Rules

### 15.1 Hold'em

For player \(i\):

$$
M_i = \operatorname{multi}(K_i \cup B) + P.
$$

The high score is:

$$
S_i = Eval_{high}(M_i).
$$

Winner:

$$
Win = \operatorname*{arg\,max}_{i \in L} S_i.
$$

### 15.2 Omaha 8B Label, Engine-Conformant High Rule

The current engine enforces the Omaha private-card quota and evaluates high
only. Let \(K_i\) have four cards. Let the public context be:

$$
C = B \cup P.
$$

The legal Omaha selections for player \(i\) are:

$$
\Omega_i
=
\left\{
A \cup G:
A \subseteq K_i,\ |A|=2,\
G \subseteq C,\ |G|=3
\right\}.
$$

The score is:

$$
S_i
=
\max_{M \in \Omega_i} Eval_{high}(\operatorname{multi}(M)).
$$

Winner:

$$
Win = \operatorname*{arg\,max}_{i \in L} S_i.
$$

### 15.3 Omaha 8B, Idealized Split-Pot Extension

The mathematically complete 8-or-better low qualifier is:

$$
Qual_8(M)
\iff
|M|=5
\land
|\operatorname{rank}(M)|=5
\land
\max(\operatorname{rank}_{A5}(M)) \le 8.
$$

The low score of a qualifying hand is the decreasing rank vector:

$$
Low(M)=
\operatorname{sort}_{desc}
\left(
\operatorname{rank}_{A5}(c):c\in M
\right).
$$

Smaller lexicographic low vectors are better. An ideal Omaha 8B split-pot rule
would define:

$$
S_i^{hi} = \max_{M \in \Omega_i} Eval_{high}(M),
$$

$$
S_i^{lo} =
\min_{\substack{M \in \Omega_i\\ Qual_8(M)}} Low(M),
$$

with no low winner if no player qualifies.

### 15.4 Razz

For player \(i\):

$$
C_i = K_i \cup U_i.
$$

Razz uses A-to-5 lowball. Straights and flushes do not hurt. Pairs hurt through
a duplicate penalty.

For every five-card subset \(M \subseteq C_i\), define:

$$
r(M)
=
\operatorname{sort}_{desc}
\left(
\operatorname{rank}_{A5}(c):c\in M
\right).
$$

Let

$$
p(M)=5-|\{\operatorname{rank}_{A5}(c):c\in M\}|.
$$

The Razz score is:

$$
S_i
=
\min_{\substack{M \subseteq C_i\\ |M|=5}}
\left(
p(M),\ r(M)
\right).
$$

Winner:

$$
Win=\operatorname*{arg\,min}_{i \in L} S_i.
$$

The persistent pool does not currently enter Razz hand strength. It still enters
the public-memory task and the next-hand pool topology.

### 15.5 Seven Card Stud High

For player \(i\):

$$
M_i
=
\operatorname{multi}(K_i \cup U_i) + P.
$$

The high score is:

$$
S_i=Eval_{high}(M_i).
$$

Winner:

$$
Win=\operatorname*{arg\,max}_{i \in L} S_i.
$$

### 15.6 Seven Card Stud 8B Label, Engine-Conformant Rule

The current engine-conformant behavior evaluates `stud_8b` with the same high
rule as Stud High:

$$
S_i=Eval_{high}(\operatorname{multi}(K_i \cup U_i)+P).
$$

### 15.7 Seven Card Stud 8B, Idealized Split-Pot Extension

The idealized split-pot rule mirrors Omaha 8B but uses the player's stud cards:

$$
\Sigma_i
=
\{M \subseteq K_i \cup U_i : |M|=5\}.
$$

High:

$$
S_i^{hi}
=
\max_{M \in \Sigma_i} Eval_{high}(M+P).
$$

Low:

$$
S_i^{lo}
=
\min_{\substack{M \in \Sigma_i\\ Qual_8(M)}} Low(M).
$$

As in Omaha 8B, if no low hand qualifies, the high winner receives the whole
pot.

## 16. Pot Map And Payouts

Let the hand pot be:

$$
Pot(x)=\sum_{i \in I_n} m_i.
$$

The engine-conformant H.O.R.S.E. v2 payout map currently selects one winning
seat:

$$
w \in Win
$$

and pays:

$$
pay_i
=
\begin{cases}
Pot(x), & i=w,\\
0, & i\ne w.
\end{cases}
$$

Stacks update by:

$$
s_i' = s_i + pay_i.
$$

Seats with zero stack are eliminated:

$$
E'=\{i:s_i'=0\}.
$$

The idealized future extension would replace this with a side-pot allocation
map:

$$
SidePots(m,F)
\to
\prod_j (amount_j, eligible_j)
\to
pay,
$$

and split high/low pots for \(O8,S8\).

## 17. Match-Level Dynamical System

A match of \(H\) hands is a composition:

$$
\Phi
=
\Phi_H \circ \Phi_{H-1} \circ \cdots \circ \Phi_1.
$$

Each hand map is:

$$
\Phi_h:
(s_h,P_h,\pi_h)
\mapsto
(s_{h+1},P_{h+1},\pi_{h+1}).
$$

The next button map is:

$$
\pi_{h+1}=\sigma_{live}(\pi_h),
$$

where \(\sigma_{live}\) is the next seat with positive stack.

The match terminates under a hand-limit rule when:

$$
h>H.
$$

It terminates under a survival rule when:

$$
|\{i:s_i>0\}|\le 1.
$$

## 18. Benchmark Metrics As Functionals

A run produces a transcript path:

$$
\tau=(x_0,\alpha_0,x_1,\alpha_1,\dots,x_T).
$$

Metrics are functionals on \(\tau\).

Hands played:

$$
M_{hands}(\tau)=H.
$$

Win count:

$$
M_{win}(i)
=
\sum_{h=1}^{H}
\mathbf{1}\{i \in Win_h\}.
$$

Win rate:

$$
M_{wr}(i)
=
\frac{M_{win}(i)}{H}.
$$

Memory accuracy:

$$
M_{mem}
=
\frac{1}{T}
\sum_{k=1}^{T}
\operatorname{Acc}(\widehat{P}_{a_k},P_k).
$$

Reset rate:

$$
M_{reset}
=
\frac{1}{H}
\sum_{h=1}^{H}
\mathbf{1}\{\delta_h=\mathsf{reset}\}.
$$

Average pool size:

$$
M_{pool}
=
\frac{1}{H}
\sum_{h=1}^{H}
\|P_h\|_1.
$$

Chip delta:

$$
M_{\Delta chips}(i)
=
s_{i,H+1}-s_{i,1}.
$$

## 19. Topological Interpretation Of The Custom Difficulty

The benchmark's custom difficulty is not merely that the rules rotate. It is
that the player must act on a path through changing quotient spaces.

At each variant \(v\), the player sees:

$$
O_i^v = X^v / \sim_i
$$

where

$$
x \sim_i x'
\iff
\Pi_i(x)=\Pi_i(x').
$$

The rotation map changes the fiber geometry:

$$
O_i^{H}
\to
O_i^{O8}
\to
O_i^{Rz}
\to
O_i^{St}
\to
O_i^{S8}.
$$

The persistent pool \(P\) creates a cross-hand coupling:

$$
X_h^v
\not\perp
X_{h+1}^{v'}
$$

because \(P_{h+1}\) is a deterministic function of \(P_h\), visible cards, and
winner metacognition.

In ordinary H.O.R.S.E., changing variants tests rule switching. In
PersistentPoker-Bench H.O.R.S.E. v2, changing variants tests rule switching plus
topological memory transport:

$$
(rule,\ observation,\ memory)
\mapsto
(rule',\ observation',\ memory').
$$

## 20. Edge Cases And Determinism

### 20.1 Duplicate Persistent Cards

If card \(c\) appears in the persistent pool with multiplicity \(k\), then it
contributes \(k\) separate instances to the custom high evaluator.

Formally:

$$
P(c)=k
\implies
c \text{ has } k \text{ copies in } Eval_{high}.
$$

### 20.2 All-In Runout

If betting ends before showdown because all live players are all-in or only one
player can act, the engine advances streets until showdown without further
decisions.

### 20.3 Folded Players And Stud Up-Cards

The engine-conformant pool update for stud-family variants appends the visible
up-cards stored on every player state. Since up-cards are public once dealt, this
is naturally modeled as:

$$
W_h = \sum_i \operatorname{multi}(U_i).
$$

### 20.4 Ties In Current H.O.R.S.E. v2 Showdown

The current H.O.R.S.E. v2 terminal resolver selects a single best player by scan
order. The idealized mathematical rule would return the full argmax or argmin
set \(Win\) and then apply side-pot or split-pot logic.

### 20.5 Bring-In Rank Ties

When multiple players have equal bring-in ranks, the deterministic seat-order
choice is:

$$
\operatorname*{arg\,min}_{i \in C} i.
$$

A future fully authentic Stud rule may use suit order.

## 22. Minimal Formal Definition

The full game can be summarized as the tuple:

$$
\mathcal{G}_{PPB-HORSE}
=
\left(
D,\ I_n,\ V,\ \rho,\ X,\ (\Pi_i)_{i\in I_n},\
\mathcal{A},\ \Lambda,\ T,\ \Gamma,\
Eval,\ Pool,\ Tie,\ Metrics
\right),
$$

where:

$$
T = \{T_\alpha:\alpha\in\mathcal{A}\}
$$

is the family of legal action transition maps,

$$
\Gamma=\{\Gamma_v:v\in V\}
$$

is the family of street advancement maps,

$$
Eval=\{Eval_H,Eval_{O8},Eval_{Rz},Eval_{St},Eval_{S8}\}
$$

is the variant-specific showdown family,

$$
Pool(P_h,W_h,\delta_h)=P_{h+1}
$$

is the persistent-pool update operator, and

$$
Metrics
$$

is the vector of benchmark functionals applied to the transcript path.

This formal object is finite-state for any fixed hand cap, stack cap, and player
count. It is therefore exactly simulable, deterministic under fixed seeds and
agent decisions, and suitable for leaderboard comparison.
